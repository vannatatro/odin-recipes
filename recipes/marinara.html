Marinara

Description

This marinara sauce is truely the best thing I have ever eaten! I have made this recipe countless times and still keep it in my weekly rotation. This sauce is so good that you must serve it with some scarpetta - a piece of bread used to mop up the remnants on the plate!

Ingredients

  • 2 (14.5 ounce) cans stewed tomatoes
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 4 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 6 tablespoons olive oil
  • Half of a finely diced onion
  • ½ cup white wine
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Over medium heat, add olive oil into a saucepan. Once shimmering, add in the finely diced onion and sauté until just starting to develope some color. This should take about 5 minutes.
  2. Add minced garlic and crushed red pepper flakes. Once fragrant (about 1 minute), add the tomato paste. Stir frequently until it has darkened slightly, about 5 minutes.
  3. Gradually stir in the white wine, making sure to create a smooth base for the sauce. Cook for 3-5 minutes. Then, add in both cans of stewed tomatoes. Season with oregano, salt, and black pepper. Bring this mixture to a simmer, then cover and let cook for 12 minutes.
  4. Add in the fresh parsley and serve.
